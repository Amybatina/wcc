Mary Anning

She sells sea-shells on the sea-shore, The shells she sells are sea-shells, I'm sure For if she sells sea-shells on the sea-shore Then I'm sure she sells sea-shore shells.

Mary Anning was a self-taught fossil collector who is most well known for all the important items she found. In addition to the ichthyosaurs, she found a plesiosaurs, a pterodactyl and hundreds of other fossils. Her finds made primarily in the Jurassic marine fossil beds along the English Channel helped scientists form ideas about prehistoric life and the history of the Earth. Her story was the inspiration for the tongue twister "She sells seashelss on the seashore".

Interesting Facts

  1. She taught herself anatomy, geology, paleontology and scientific expression.
  2. Mary was well known and consulted on issues of anatomy as well as fossils, in Britain, Europe and America.
  3. Her only scientific writing to be published appeared in Magazine of Natual History in 1839.
  4. Charles Dickens wrote an article about her life in 1865.
